How to join the cycle

by 0xSnipe
31/01/20

Understanding states of the verifier

When initially setting up a verifier and sentinel you may wonder when you're going to earn rewards for joining the network. The verifier can be in one of three states:

  • In queue - not eligible for selection
  • In queue - eligible for selection
  • In cycle - actively contributing to the network
The state of your verifier depends on a multitude of factors, we talk about them down below.

In queue - not eligible for selection

When you first join the network, your verifier will belong to the first category.
The network actively enforces a 30-day incubation period, this means that your node will have to reside in the queue for at least 30 days before the network will even begin to consider letting your node join the network.

This is a protection mechanism put in place to discourage the usage of botnet(s), proxies or illicitly funded servers to rapidly gain an advantage in joining the network.

In queue - eligible for selection

After the incubation period is over your node will become eligible to join the network and it becomes a matter of statistical probability to know how long it may take to successfully join the network.

The network utilizes a random selection procedure - based on the 50-block incremented block hash. What this means is that the network will switch the verifier it is actively voting for every 50 blocks. This does not mean that a new verifier joins the network every 50 blocks, as the blockchain rules ensure that only a few nodes are able to join every day. The verifier which is chosen is deterministically random, based on the block hash.

The interval between new verifier additions to the network is regulated by a formula. This formula uses the amount of verifiers in the cycle to determine when a new verifier is allowed to join. The bigger the cycle is, the more time has to pass to allow a new entrant to join the network.

The amount of new additions per day is an important metric to consider when deciding how many verifiers to add to the queue, as it has an impact on your propensity to join the network.

Other metrics which have an impact on this propensity are:

  • Competition: other people are trying to join the network as well, the amount of queue verifiers which are eligible for joining will largely determine your odds
  • Verifier efficiency: not all nodes in the queue are the same and will vary in quality, the more your node is resilient against DDoS attacks, the more chance you have of joining the network - this is a small concern, as the sentinel is able to join the network for you when your node is unresponsive - it speaks for itself that this causes some kind of delay, which you may want to account for and avoid at all
Using these variables, it is possible to get an idea of how much it costs for a node to join the cycle, and to calculate a break-even point based on the rewards the network gives you.

In cycle - actively contributing to the network

Congratulations, you made it!

How much Nyzo will I earn?

All transactions incur a 0.25% fee. This fee is split evenly among the verifier of this block and the verifiers of the previous nine blocks. Until a lot of people have a lot of coins, organic transaction volume will not be high enough to make transaction verification worthwhile.

To remedy this, the developers have created 27 million seed transactions: one transaction per block for the first six years that the system will be in operation. These transactions are drawn from an account for which the private key was generated, used to sign the transaction and discarded without saving.

Release 479 adds protection to the seed-funding wallet, this update ensures that no other transactions originating from the seed wallet can be included by the network.

The SeedTransactionManager automatically includes seed transactions into the blocks your verifier is producing.
